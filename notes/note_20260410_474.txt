KEY CONCEPT

Backtesting is a crucial part of algorithmic trading because it allows traders to test their ideas and strategies in a simulated environment before risking real money. This helps to avoid emotional decisions based on short-term market movements, and instead allows traders to focus on long-term goals and risk management. When it comes to designing a strategy, it's essential to consider metrics such as Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio, and Drawdown to get a comprehensive picture of its performance. A backtesting library like backtrader handles the heavy lifting, allowing me to focus on developing and refining my strategy.
